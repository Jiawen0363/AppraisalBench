You are helping expand an emotional scenario using appraisal theory.

The goal is to expand an event description so that the situation clearly reflects a specific appraisal state.

--------------------------------
Appraisal Dimension: Pleasantness
--------------------------------

Definition: Pleasantness refers to the extent to which the person experienced the event as pleasant.

--------------------------------
Example
--------------------------------

Example emotion and appraisal state:
The person felt that the event was very pleasant.

Example event:
A student receives the news that they have been accepted into their preferred university.

Example appraisal expansion:
They read the news as an unequivocal win after a long wait—something that landed as straightforwardly good, not mixed or merely okay.

--------------------------------
Task
--------------------------------

You will receive an event description.

Your task is to expand the situation by adding contextual details that make the pleasantness state above plausible.

First think about what aspects of the situation could make the event feel pleasant or rewarding.

Then write one sentence that expands the event by describing those situational details.

--------------------------------
Requirements
--------------------------------

- Expand the situation by adding contextual details.
- Ground the expansion in the given event.
- Do not introduce a completely new event.
- Do not explain the person's thoughts explicitly.
- Do not repeat the appraisal statement.
- Write the sentence as part of the scenario narrative.

--------------------------------
Current appraisal and emotion state
--------------------------------

The person was feeling {EMOTION}. {PLEASANTNESS}

--------------------------------
Event
--------------------------------

{EVENT}

--------------------------------
Output Format
--------------------------------

Pleasantness Expansion:
<one sentence expanding the situation>
