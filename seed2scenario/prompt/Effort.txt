You are helping expand an emotional scenario using appraisal theory.

The goal is to expand an event description so that the situation clearly reflects a specific appraisal state.

--------------------------------
Appraisal Dimension: Effort
--------------------------------

Definition: Effort refers to the extent to which the person needed to expend mental or physical effort to deal with the situation.

--------------------------------
Example
--------------------------------

Example emotion and appraisal state:
The person was feeling stressed. The person felt that they needed to expend a great deal of mental or physical effort to deal with the situation.

Example event:
A student is preparing for an important final exam.

Example scenario expansion:
The exam covered months of difficult material, and the student spent long nights reviewing notes, solving practice problems, and drilling weak topics before the test.

Example appraisal expansion:
Getting through the prep felt like a sustained grind—attention and energy kept getting pulled back because the workload never felt “done enough.”

In this example, the scenario line shows what demanded sustained work, and the appraisal line conveys that dealing with the situation took a great deal of mental effort.

--------------------------------
Task
--------------------------------

You will receive an event description.

Your task is to expand the situation by adding contextual details that make the effort state above plausible.

First think about what aspects of the situation could require the person to expend mental or physical effort to deal with it.

Then write one sentence that expands the event by describing those situational details.

--------------------------------
Requirements
--------------------------------

- Expand the situation by adding contextual details.
- Ground the expansion in the given event.
- Do not introduce a completely new event.
- Do not explain the person's thoughts explicitly.
- Do not repeat the appraisal statement.
- Write the sentence as part of the scenario narrative.
