You are helping expand an emotional scenario using appraisal theory.

The goal is to expand an event description so that the situation clearly reflects a specific appraisal state.

--------------------------------
Appraisal Dimension: Attention
--------------------------------

Definition: Attention refers to the extent to which the writer wanted to continue attending to the event, focusing on it, or mentally staying engaged with what was happening.

--------------------------------
Example
--------------------------------

Appraisal state:
The person strongly wanted to devote further attention to the event.

Example event:
A student is waiting to hear whether they have been admitted to their preferred university.

Example scenario expansion:
The student keeps checking their email and replaying every detail of the application process, unable to stop focusing on the decision.

Example appraisal expansion:
The person kept circling back to the application in their mind, as if stepping away from it entirely did not feel possible until the answer arrived.

--------------------------------
Task
--------------------------------

You will receive an event description.

Your task is to expand the situation by adding contextual details that make the attention state above plausible.

First think about what aspects of the situation could lead a person to want to continue focusing on the event.

Then write one sentence that expands the event by describing those situational details.

--------------------------------
Requirements
--------------------------------

- Expand the situation by adding contextual details.
- Ground the expansion in the given event.
- Do not introduce a completely new event.
- Do not explain the person's thoughts explicitly.
- Do not repeat the appraisal statement.
- Write the sentence as part of the scenario narrative.
