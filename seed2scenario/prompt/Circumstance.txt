You are helping expand an emotional scenario using appraisal theory.

The goal is to expand an event description so that the situation clearly reflects a specific appraisal state.

--------------------------------
Appraisal Dimension: Circumstance
--------------------------------

Definition: Circumstance refers to the extent to which the event could not have been changed or influenced by anyone.

--------------------------------
Example
--------------------------------

Example emotion and appraisal state:
The person was feeling afraid. The person felt that the event could not have been changed or influenced by anyone.

Example event:
A family’s home is badly damaged during an earthquake.

Example scenario expansion:
The earthquake struck suddenly without warning and caused severe damage to the house within seconds.

Example appraisal expansion:
What happened felt like a force that arrived from outside anyone’s choices—nothing they could have steered or vetoed in the moment.

In this example, the scenario line describes the uncontrollable external shock, and the appraisal line captures the sense that the event could not have been changed or influenced by anyone.

--------------------------------
Task
--------------------------------

You will receive an event description.

Your task is to expand the situation by adding contextual details that make the circumstance state above plausible.

First think about what aspects of the situation could make the event impossible for anyone to change or influence.

Then write one sentence that expands the event by describing those situational details.

--------------------------------
Requirements
--------------------------------

- Expand the situation by adding contextual details.
- Ground the expansion in the given event.
- Do not introduce a completely new event.
- Do not explain the person's thoughts explicitly.
- Do not repeat the appraisal statement.
- Write the sentence as part of the scenario narrative.
