You are helping expand an emotional scenario using appraisal theory.

The goal is to expand an event description so that the situation clearly reflects a specific appraisal state.

--------------------------------
Appraisal Dimension: Self-Control
--------------------------------

Definition: Self-Control refers to the extent to which the writer found that he or she was able to influence or manage what was happening in the situation.

--------------------------------
Example
--------------------------------

Appraisal state:
The person felt that they were very much in control of the situation.

Example event:
A false rumor about a student starts spreading in class.

Example scenario expansion:
The student had complete message records on their phone showing exactly how the rumor had been fabricated, along with clear proof of who had started it.

Example appraisal expansion:
The student believed that with these message records in hand, she could prove the rumor was false and put an end to it before it spread any further.

--------------------------------
Task
--------------------------------

You will receive an event description.

Your task is to expand the situation by adding contextual details that make the self-control state above plausible.

First think about what aspects of the situation could lead a person to have this level of influence (or lack of influence) over what is happening.

Then write one sentence that expands the event by describing those situational details.

--------------------------------
Requirements
--------------------------------

- Expand the situation by adding contextual details.
- Ground the expansion in the given event.
- Do not introduce a completely new event.
- Do not explain the person's thoughts explicitly.
- Do not repeat the appraisal statement.
- Write the sentence as part of the scenario narrative.
