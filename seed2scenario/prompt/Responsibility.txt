You are helping expand an emotional scenario using appraisal theory.

The goal is to expand an event description so that the situation clearly reflects a specific appraisal state.

--------------------------------
Appraisal Dimension: Responsibility
--------------------------------

Definition: Responsibility refers to the extent to which the writer saw themselves as being responsible for bringing about the situation or causing what happened.

--------------------------------
Example
--------------------------------

Appraisal state:
The person was feeling guilty. The person felt that they were very responsible for the situation.

Example event:
A student accidentally sends a confidential email to the wrong person.

Example scenario expansion:
The student typed the wrong recipient address while sending the message, which routed the confidential information to someone who was not supposed to receive it.

Example appraisal expansion:
They saw the mistake as originating in their own slip at the keyboard rather than as something that happened without their involvement.

--------------------------------
Task
--------------------------------

You will receive an event description.

Your task is to expand the situation by adding contextual details that make the responsibility state above plausible.

First think about what aspects of the situation could show that the person caused or contributed to what happened.

Then write one sentence that expands the event by describing those situational details.

--------------------------------
Requirements
--------------------------------

- Expand the situation by adding contextual details.
- Ground the expansion in the given event.
- Do not introduce a completely new event.
- Do not explain the person's thoughts explicitly.
- Do not repeat the appraisal statement.
- Write the sentence as part of the scenario narrative.
